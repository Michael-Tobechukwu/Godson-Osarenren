About Godson Osarenren

Godson Osarenren is the author of "The Broken Cross", Poet, Teacher, Blogger and a courageous voice for human rights and social justice.

He is a Nigerian confessionist poet whose work is characterized by a unique blend of traditional African oral poetry and modernist techniques. His poetry is known for its musicality, imagery, and use of metaphors.

One of the key features of Osarenren's poetic style is his use of repetition and rhythm. His poems often feature a strong musical quality, with repeated phrases and a consistent beat that mimics the rhythms of traditional African folk songs.

Osarenren's poetry also frequently incorporates vivid imagery and striking metaphors. He uses these devices to explore themes such as identity, love, and the complexities of human relationships.

Another notable aspect of Osarenren's style is his use of language. He often employs a mix of English, French and Nigerian pidgin, as well as other Nigerian languages such as Edo, Yoruba, and Igbo. This multilingual approach reflects Osarenren's commitment to celebrating the diversity of Nigerian culture and language.

Overall, Godson Osarenren's poetic style is characterized by a rich blend of traditional and modern techniques, drawing on the oral traditions of African poetry while also incorporating contemporary literary techniques.

His philosophy about life is; 'Though the sky is far off, no one has ever borrowed the wings of a bird'.
